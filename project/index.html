The way he sees it, she wanted it. The way she sees it, he raped her.

When two sophomores had sex last fall, it was one of countless drunken hookups that happen on IU’s campus on any given night. But their encounter shows the gray area students wade through with hookups, especially when it comes to consent — trafficking in mixed signals, alcohol and social pressures. It raises questions about the ways we define consent, in our institutions and in our own bedrooms and dorms.

Marion Zerfoss, then a sophomore, reported an assault to the Bloomington Police Department last October. The assault had occurred over a week earlier, but like many assault victims, Zerfoss was initially hesitant to report, weighing the pain of what she’d gone through already against the idea of enduring the slog of the criminal justice system.

The night it happened, Zerfoss, then 20, had been pregaming at her house for her roommate’s 21st birthday. According to court documents, Zerfoss drank nine shots of Fireball in under an hour and found herself more drunk than she had ever been in her life.
